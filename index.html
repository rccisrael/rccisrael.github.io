About Our Lower Waterford Home

The Carpenter Family has had a presence in Lower Waterford for over 250+ years. Needless to say there is a lot of family history!

Wendy's Father (David) spearheaded the process of repurchasing Carpenter family land in Waterford over the past 50 years. We bought this home - just down the street from the original Carpenter family property - in 2017 and have been working on it ever since.

Located at 506 Lower Waterford Road, this property runs from the banks of the Connecticut River up to the top of our very own Holbrook Hill. The house itself was built in the 1860's and has direct ties to the Carpenter family. Its builder Charles Ross was the daughter of Hannah Carpenter and fought in the Civil War. With 250 acres to enjoy and maintain, there is a ton of fun things to do right here and plenty of projects to keep us occupied.

Waterford itself is a beautiful small town of ~1000 residents in Vermont's Northeat Kingdom bordering New Hampshire. In fact, it was recently named a Top 10 Ten prettiest mountain town in New England. The Town today is probably best known as the home of the Rabbit Hill Inn - just a short walk from the property. The small inn with a lovely restaurant consistently ranks among the Inns in America.

Though we love to spend time in Waterford, most of our New England adventures include a short drive into New Hampshire's White Mountains or Vermont's Northeast Kingdom. From skiing and hiking to eating a fun local restaurants, this region has a lot to offer "down-country" folks looking to relax.

We hope you have an amazing stay and are very excited that you are here!
